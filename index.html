20th Century Northern Ireland

36th Ulster division suffers serious losses at the Battle of the Somme in 1916, a date that enters both Nationalist and Unionist popular memory for very different reasons.

The Government of Ireland Act of 1920 was intended to make two sub-states of the United Kingdom in both Ireland and Northern Ireland, but after Sinn Féin won a majority in Ireland in the general election of 1918, a war broke out between Irish republicans and the British forces in Ireland. When the war ended, a new 'Free State' had been established in 26 counties of Ireland, but the 6 north-eastern counties that make up Northern Ireland opted to remain part of the UK.

Northern Ireland was created through an act that was never intended to be permanent, and this impermanence contributed to some of the fundamental instabilities of the state.
